Background

I'm Om, a Class 12 student on the ICSE board, appearing for my 2027 board examinations with a focus on Physics and Mathematics. My academic interest sits at the intersection of aerospace engineering, propulsion, and applied computing.

I've written independent technical papers on Nuclear Thermal Propulsion and crewed interplanetary mission architecture, and I'm building OpenMars — a mission-planning and propulsion-analysis tool — as a way to apply and test what I study.

Paper 01 · Independent · Zenodo · 2025
Nuclear Propulsion for Deep Space Exploration: A Comparative Analysis of NTP and NEP Systems and Their Feasibility for Interplanetary Travel

Compares Nuclear Thermal Propulsion and Nuclear Electric Propulsion as alternatives to chemical rockets for deep-space exploration. Outlines how NTP offers higher thrust with moderate efficiency, while NEP provides extremely high efficiency but low thrust. Evaluates engineering, performance, and mission suitability — concluding NTP is better for crewed Mars missions, NEP for long-duration robotic exploration.
