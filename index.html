// 004 — Projects

What I Have Built

OpenMars Active — v0.1

A web-based mission-planning and propulsion-analysis platform for interplanetary mission design. OpenMars models the performance trade-offs between Chemical Propulsion, Nuclear Thermal Propulsion (NTP), and Nuclear Electric Propulsion (NEP) for Earth-to-Mars trajectories. The tool computes delta-v budgets, mass ratios, and propellant requirements using the Tsiolkovsky rocket equation, and visualises results through interactive charts.

I built OpenMars to close the gap between the mission-architecture concepts I was reading about and my ability to actually calculate and compare them. It's a learning project as much as an engineering tool — every feature I add requires understanding the underlying physics well enough to implement it correctly.

// 005 — Research

Independent Research & Technical Writing

The following are independently written technical papers exploring aerospace engineering topics I've studied in depth. They are self-directed research exercises, not peer-reviewed academic publications.

Paper 01 · Independent Technical Writing · Zenodo

Nuclear Propulsion for Deep Space Exploration: A Comparative Analysis of NTP and NEP Systems and Their Feasibility for Interplanetary Travel

This paper compares Nuclear Thermal Propulsion (NTP) and Nuclear Electric Propulsion (NEP) as alternatives to chemical rockets for deep-space exploration. It outlines how NTP offers higher thrust with moderate efficiency, while NEP provides extremely high efficiency but low thrust. The study evaluates their engineering, performance, and mission suitability, showing NTP is better for crewed Mars missions and NEP for long-duration robotic exploration. It also discusses safety, policy, and regulatory challenges, and considers hybrid architectures as a potential future solution.
